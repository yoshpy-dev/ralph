## Positive notes

- The `cleanup_on_exit` rewrite is a clean, focused improvement. Removing the `_CHILD_PIDS` loop eliminates a real PID-reuse risk on long-running sessions where completed PIDs could be recycled by the OS. The .pid file strategy is inherently safe because files are deleted when slices complete.
- The explanatory comment (lines 96-99) clearly documents *why* the in-memory PID list is not used, which is good practice for a non-obvious design decision.
- The `timeout` status was added consistently across all three display functions in `ralph-status-helpers.sh`: `resolve_display_phase` (line 113), `status_icon` (line 168), and `_render_table` detail column (line 408). This is the correct scope for the fix.
- The `timeout` status was also correctly added to both terminal-status pattern lists in `ralph-orchestrator.sh` (lines 771 and 841), ensuring timeouts are properly classified as failures and don't cause infinite deferral in the scheduling loop.
